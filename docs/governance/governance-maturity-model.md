# Governance Maturity Model

## Overview

The Governance Maturity Model defines the operational maturity progression of Microsoft 365 governance capabilities within the M365 Platform Governance Framework.

The model is intended to support:
- Governance standardization
- Operational modernization
- Platform engineering maturity
- Automation lifecycle evolution
- Continuous operational improvement

---

# Maturity Levels

| Level   | Classification                  |
| ------- | ------------------------------- |
| Level 1 | Reactive                        |
| Level 2 | Standardized                    |
| Level 3 | Automated                       |
| Level 4 | Optimized                       |
| Level 5 | Enterprise Intelligent Platform |

---

# Level 1 — Reactive

## Characteristics

- Governance validation performed manually
- Limited operational visibility
- Minimal standardization
- Reactive issue resolution
- No centralized governance framework

## Operational Risks

- High configuration drift
- Inconsistent governance enforcement
- Increased operational overhead
- Limited auditability

## Typical Environment Indicators

- Manual PowerShell execution
- Ad-hoc reporting
- No remediation workflows
- No governance KPIs

---

# Level 2 — Standardized

## Characteristics

- Governance standards formally documented
- Operational reporting introduced
- Centralized governance procedures established
- Basic automation workflows implemented

## Operational Improvements

- Improved governance consistency
- Reduced manual operational effort
- Standardized operational processes

## Typical Environment Indicators

- Governance baselines defined
- Standard operational runbooks
- Centralized logging introduced
- Structured reporting available

---

# Level 3 — Automated

## Characteristics

- Automated governance validation implemented
- Remediation orchestration introduced
- CI/CD governance controls enabled
- Operational metrics collection established

## Operational Improvements

- Reduced governance drift
- Increased operational efficiency
- Improved audit readiness
- Faster remediation lifecycle

## Typical Environment Indicators

- Automated governance audits
- Scheduled validation workflows
- Pull request governance controls
- Automated remediation simulation

---

# Level 4 — Optimized

## Characteristics

- Governance trend analytics implemented
- Executive operational dashboards available
- Technical debt reporting established
- Governance intelligence capabilities introduced

## Operational Improvements

- Improved strategic visibility
- Enhanced operational forecasting
- Governance posture trending
- Proactive operational management

## Typical Environment Indicators

- Governance scorecards
- Historical trend analysis
- Executive operational summaries
- Technical debt lifecycle tracking

---

# Level 5 — Enterprise Intelligent Platform

## Characteristics

- Full governance automation lifecycle implemented
- Advanced operational analytics enabled
- Continuous compliance validation integrated
- Intelligent governance decision support established

## Operational Improvements

- Predictive governance management
- Continuous operational optimization
- Advanced governance intelligence
- Enterprise-scale automation maturity

## Typical Environment Indicators

- Multi-tenant governance orchestration
- Predictive remediation workflows
- Intelligent governance scoring
- Enterprise analytics integration

---

# Maturity Progression Strategy

Organizations typically evolve through the maturity model by:

1. Standardizing governance processes
2. Introducing centralized operational visibility
3. Automating validation workflows
4. Implementing remediation orchestration
5. Enabling governance intelligence and analytics
6. Integrating continuous operational optimization
